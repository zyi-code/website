    WHAT IS PHOTOJOURNALISM?

    PHOTOJOURNALISM for me is an art of visual storytelling that goes
    beyond capturing images and captions. It involves carefully
    curated collections of images that create a powerful narrative,
    revealing the unspoken stories beneath the surface.


    The mastery of a photojournalist lies in their ability
    to select, arrange, and present images that
    build a narrative, guiding the viewer on a journey of
    discovery. For example, a photo essay documenting
    the aftermath of a natural disaster can reveal the
    resilience, loss, and enduring human spirit in the face of
    adversity. Photojournalism is not just about
    capturing aesthetically pleasing images; it's
    about harnessing the power of photography to illuminate
    the human experience, uncover truths, and forge
    emotional connections with the audience.
